Safe Break-in
process

Safe liberation throught break-in

In some cases, banks may need to forcibly open closed safes within their branches. This procedure is complex and risky due to various legal obligations. Currently, BNP Paribas relies on a manual process, which can lead to inefficiencies and expose the bank to compliance risks.

As a UX team, we were tasked with designing a solution to unify the process for safe break-ins and minimize the risk of procedural non-compliance through thoughtful design.

See case study

Design steps

The design process for revamping Impôt.gouv involved several key stages, each aimed at refining user experience and ensuring accessibility for everyone.

1.

Research - concept map

We analyzed existing processes and created a concept map that outlined the challenges and needs of employees involved in safe break-ins.

2.

Ideation

Various design solutions were explored, focusing on how to make the process both secure and efficient while keeping the user experience simple.

3.

Sketching

Initial wireframes and layouts were designed, focusing on key features like secure authentication, process tracking, and reporting.

4.

Prototyping

An interactive prototype was built, allowing stakeholders to test the flow and provide feedback before moving into development.
