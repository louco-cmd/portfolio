Document management in loan processes

In the context of client-bank contractual processes, banking advisors handle a large set of documents and supporting evidence. The existing tools for document classification and management do not meet user needs, leading to process circumvention and the massive storage of client documents on employee devices.

The goal was to redesign the interface and user experience of the document management tools (eDoc) to align with employee needs, reduce their frustrations, and improve operational efficiency.

Research - experience map builing

For this project, we conducted interviews with 6 bank advisors to better understand their pain points and needs when managing documents. The insights gathered were mapped into an experience map, highlighting key challenges like inefficient document processing and navigation issues. This allowed us to visualize the user journey and identify critical areas for improvement.As designers, we played a crucial role in helping the project team prioritize development tasks using the MoSCoW method. This approach ensured that the most impactful features, such as batch document imports and AI-assisted verification, were addressed first.

Priorization

Priorization

Using insights gathered from stakeholders and research, I applied a feature prioritization framework.The focus was on delivering value early while addressing high-impact areas. This led to a roadmap where critical functionalities, such as faster document uploads and robust search features, were prioritized over less impactful features.

User testing

User testing

We conducted three user tests in a controlled environment, following a detailed protocol we prepared in advance. These tests allowed us to observe how real users interacted with the prototype, ensuring that the features we designed met their expectations. The feedback we gathered helped identify areas for improvement, particularly in terms of navigation and document verification workflows. By iterating on the design based on these insights, we ensured the final solution was aligned with users’ needs and improved their efficiency.
